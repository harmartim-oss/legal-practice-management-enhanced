# Northeast Ontario Court Requirements Research
## Consolidated Practice Direction for the Northeast Region

**Effective Date**: May 1, 2026  
**Region**: Northeast Ontario (Sault Ste. Marie, Sudbury, North Bay, Cochrane, Timmins, Haileybury, Parry Sound, Gore Bay)  
**Source**: Ontario Superior Court of Justice

---

## Key Regional Requirements for Sault Ste. Marie

### 1. Presumptive Modes of Appearance

**Family Proceedings:**
- Case conferences: Via Zoom
- Trial management conferences: Via Zoom
- Settlement conferences: In person
- Binding Judicial Dispute Resolution: In person

**Criminal Proceedings:**
- Bail hearings: In person
- Bail review and detention reviews: In person
- Pre-trial applications: In person

**Bankruptcy Matters:**
- All bankruptcy matters: In writing or video conference
- Discretion available for in-person hearings on case-by-case basis

### 2. Scheduling with Calendly

**Sault Ste. Marie Calendly Link**: https://calendly.com/saultstemarie-scj

**Matters Scheduled via Calendly:**
- Family case conferences
- Family settlement conferences
- Family trial management conferences
- Civil judicial pre-trial conferences

**Trial Coordinator Contact**: SaultSteMarie.scj.tc@ontario.ca

### 3. Civil Proceedings Requirements

#### Short Motions
- Expected duration: One hour or less
- Scheduled on regular motions days
- Materials served and filed within Rules of Civil Procedure timelines
- Associate judges have jurisdiction except for matters specified in Rule 37.02(2)

#### Long Motions
- Expected duration: More than one hour
- Require special date scheduling
- Protocol:
  1. Initially made returnable on regular motions list
  2. Parties attend first return date to set timetable or file consent timetable
  3. In week prior to next return date, counsel contacts trial coordinator to canvass dates
  4. At second return date, presiding judge confirms materials filed and confirms hearing date
  5. Once date scheduled, may only be vacated or adjourned by order of judge or associate judge

#### Factums and Books of Authority
- Required on long motions
- Optional on short motions (subject to court direction)
- Must comply with Consolidated Provincial Practice Direction requirements
- Failure to comply may result in motion not being heard

#### Page Limits
- Factums: 15 pages maximum
- Judicial pre-trial conference briefs: 15 pages maximum

#### Mortgage Proceedings
- Designated places for commencement: North Bay, Parry Sound, Sudbury, Haileybury, Sault Ste. Marie, Cochrane, or Gore Bay
- Applies to property located anywhere in Northeast Region
- Pursuant to Rule 13.1.01(3) of Rules of Civil Procedure

#### Construction Liens
- In Sudbury: Associate judges hear construction lien matters (except those with exclusive judge jurisdiction)
- Throughout region: Motions and hearings with reference to associate judge heard by associate judge

#### Case Conferences
- Associate judges authorized to convene case conferences
- Associate judges authorized to make case management orders
- Separate judge's order not required for each proceeding
- Applies to all matters except construction lien cases (Rule 50.13)

#### First Judicial Pre-Trial Conference
- Preferred attendance: Counsel who will appear at trial and their clients
- Objectives: Discuss resolution at first appearance
- If settlement not achieved, judge/associate judge shall order:
  - Exchange of Affidavits of Documents with copies
  - Date for examinations and answering undertakings
  - Date for pre-trial conference
  - Other necessary directions

### 4. Family Proceedings Requirements

**Motions:**
- Short motions: One hour or less
- Long motions: More than one hour (require special date)

- Specific scheduling and appearance requirements

**Page Limits:**

**Trial Readiness Hearings:**

### 6. Divisional Court Proceedings

## Key Compliance Points for Tim Harmar Legal

### 1. Scheduling Requirements
- Use Calendly for family case conferences, settlement conferences, trial management conferences, and civil pre-trial conferences
- Contact trial coordinator for long motions scheduling
- Maintain awareness of presumptive modes of appearance (Zoom vs. in-person)

### 2. Document Preparation
- Factums limited to 15 pages
- Pre-trial briefs limited to 15 pages
- Proper formatting and compliance with Provincial Practice Directions required
- Books of authority must comply with specified requirements

### 3. Motion Practice
- Short motions (≤1 hour): Regular motions days
- Long motions (>1 hour): Special date scheduling via trial coordinator
- Associate judges available for most motions
- Proper materials preparation and timely filing essential

### 4. Appearance Modes
- Family proceedings: Primarily Zoom (except BJDR and settlement conferences)
- Criminal proceedings: Primarily in-person
- Request changes to presumptive mode by writing to trial coordinator

### 5. Regional Variations
- Sault Ste. Marie has specific procedures for mortgage proceedings
- Calendly scheduling available for major family and civil matters
- Trial coordinators available for scheduling assistance
