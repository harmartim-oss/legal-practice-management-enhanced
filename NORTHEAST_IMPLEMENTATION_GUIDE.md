# Northeast Ontario Regional Compliance Implementation Guide
## Tim Harmar Legal Practice Management System

**Document Version**: 1.0  
**Effective Date**: May 4, 2026  
**Region Focus**: Northeast Ontario (Sault Ste. Marie, Sudbury, North Bay, Cochrane, Timmins, Haileybury, Parry Sound, Gore Bay)

---

## Executive Summary

This implementation guide outlines the specific requirements for integrating Northeast Ontario court procedures, Rules of Civil Procedure compliance, and Court Practice Directions into the Tim Harmar Legal Practice Management System. The guide ensures that all bills of costs, invoices, and legal documents generated by the system comply with regional court requirements.

The application must allow users to select the specific Northeast Ontario court location for each matter. This selection determines which regional procedures, contact information, and scheduling requirements apply.

**Available Court Locations:**
- Sault Ste. Marie (Primary location for Tim Harmar)
- Sudbury
- North Bay
- Cochrane
- Timmins
- Haileybury
- Parry Sound
- Gore Bay/Manitoulin

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Add court location dropdown to matter creation form
- Store court location with each matter
- Use court location to determine applicable procedures
- Display court-specific contact information and calendly links
- Generate location-specific compliance reports

## Part 2: Document Compliance Requirements

### 2.1 Page Limits for Regional Courts

The Northeast Region Practice Direction specifies strict page limits for certain documents. The application must enforce these limits and warn users when documents exceed limits.

**Document Page Limits:**
- Factums: 15 pages maximum
- Judicial pre-trial conference briefs: 15 pages maximum
- Motion briefs: 15 pages maximum
- Trial briefs: 15 pages maximum

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Add page limit validation to document generation
- Display page count in real-time as documents are created
- Warn users when approaching page limits
- Prevent generation of documents exceeding limits
- Provide guidance on condensing content

### 2.2 Factum Compliance

Factums must comply with the Consolidated Provincial Practice Direction for Civil Proceedings. The system must validate factum structure and formatting.

**Required Factum Elements:**
- Caption (case name, court, file number)
- Statement of facts
- Issues
- Law
- Analysis
- Conclusion
- Proper formatting and font size (minimum 11 point)
- Page numbering
- Table of contents (for longer factums)
- Table of authorities

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Create factum template with all required elements
- Validate factum structure before generation
- Enforce formatting requirements
- Generate compliance checklist
- Provide factum writing guidance

### 2.3 Bill of Costs Compliance

Bills of costs must comply with Form 57A for civil proceedings and Rule 24 for family proceedings. Regional variations must be applied.

**Regional Compliance Considerations:**
- Proper court location designation
- Correct trial coordinator contact information
- Compliance with regional page limits
- Regional fee schedules (where applicable)
- Proper certification by lawyer

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Add regional court location to bill of costs
- Include trial coordinator contact information
- Apply regional fee schedules
- Validate compliance with regional requirements
- Generate regional compliance report

## Part 3: Motion Practice Procedures

### 3.1 Short Motion Procedures

Short motions (expected duration one hour or less) are scheduled on regular motions days. The system must track motion duration and apply appropriate procedures.

**Short Motion Requirements:**
- Expected duration: One hour or less
- Scheduled on regular motions days
- Materials served and filed within Rules of Civil Procedure timelines
- Associate judges have jurisdiction (except Rule 37.02(2) exceptions)
- No special scheduling required

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Add motion duration field to motion tracking
- Classify motions as short or long based on duration
- Generate short motion checklists
- Track filing deadlines
- Provide motion materials templates

### 3.2 Long Motion Procedures

Long motions (expected duration more than one hour) require special date scheduling following a specific protocol. The system must track each step of the protocol.

**Long Motion Protocol:**
1. Motion initially made returnable on regular motions list
2. Parties attend first return date to set timetable or file consent timetable
3. In week prior to next return date, counsel contacts trial coordinator to canvass dates
4. At second return date, presiding judge confirms materials filed and confirms hearing date
5. Once date scheduled, may only be vacated or adjourned by order of judge or associate judge

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Track long motion scheduling protocol steps
- Generate reminders for each protocol step
- Maintain timetable with key dates
- Track trial coordinator communication
- Generate long motion status reports
- Enforce protocol compliance

### 3.3 Motion Materials Preparation

The system must ensure all motion materials comply with regional requirements.

**Motion Materials Requirements:**
- Factums (required for long motions, optional for short motions)
- Books of authority
- Affidavits and exhibits
- Factums limited to 15 pages
- Proper formatting and citations
- Compliance with Provincial Practice Direction

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Create motion materials templates
- Validate factum compliance
- Check page limits
- Verify proper formatting
- Generate materials checklist
- Provide compliance guidance

## Part 4: Appearance Modes and Scheduling

### 4.1 Presumptive Modes of Appearance

Different types of proceedings have different presumptive modes of appearance (in-person vs. Zoom). The system must track these and alert users to changes.

**Family Proceedings Presumptive Modes:**
- Case conferences: Zoom
- Trial management conferences: Zoom
- Settlement conferences: In person
- Binding Judicial Dispute Resolution: In person

**Criminal Proceedings Presumptive Modes:**
- Bail hearings: In person
- Bail review and detention reviews: In person
- Pre-trial applications: In person

**Bankruptcy Matters Presumptive Modes:**
- All bankruptcy matters: In writing or video conference
- Discretion for in-person on case-by-case basis

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Store presumptive mode for each proceeding type
- Display presumptive mode in matter details
- Alert users to mode changes
- Track requests for mode changes
- Maintain record of approved mode changes
- Generate appearance mode compliance reports

### 4.2 Mode Change Requests

Parties may request changes to presumptive modes by writing to the trial coordinator. The system must track these requests and their status.

**Mode Change Request Process:**
- Write to trial coordinator at location-specific email
- Include matter details and reason for change
- Request must be made in advance of scheduled appearance
- Trial coordinator approves or denies request
- Confirmation of approved mode change

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Create mode change request template
- Track mode change requests and status
- Generate trial coordinator communication templates
- Maintain record of approved mode changes
- Alert users to mode changes
- Generate compliance report of mode changes

## Part 5: Regional Variations and Special Procedures

### 5.1 Mortgage Proceedings

Mortgage proceedings have designated places for commencement in the Northeast Region.

**Designated Places for Mortgage Proceedings:**
- North Bay
- Parry Sound
- Sudbury
- Haileybury
- Sault Ste. Marie
- Cochrane
- Gore Bay

**Applicable to:** Property located anywhere in Northeast Region

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Identify mortgage proceedings in matter type
- Restrict commencement to designated locations
- Provide guidance on proper location selection
- Generate compliance report for mortgage proceedings
- Track mortgage proceedings separately

### 5.2 Construction Lien Matters

Construction lien matters have special procedures and jurisdiction rules in the Northeast Region.

**Construction Lien Procedures:**
- In Sudbury: Associate judges hear construction lien matters (except exclusive judge jurisdiction)
- Throughout region: Motions and hearings with reference to associate judge heard by associate judge
- Case conferences not subject to associate judge authorization (Rule 50.13)

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Identify construction lien matters
- Track associate judge jurisdiction rules
- Generate construction lien-specific procedures
- Provide construction lien compliance guidance
- Track construction lien matters separately

### 5.3 Remote Locations (Chapleau and Gogama)

Criminal proceedings originating in Chapleau and Gogama have special procedures due to remote location.

**Special Procedures for Remote Locations:**
- Specific scheduling requirements
- Appearance mode considerations
- Trial readiness procedures
- Communication protocols

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Identify matters from remote locations
- Apply special procedures automatically
- Generate remote location compliance reports
- Track communication with remote locations
- Provide remote location guidance

## Part 6: Case Management and Conferences

### 6.1 Case Conferences

Associate judges are authorized to convene case conferences and make case management orders in the Northeast Region (except construction lien cases under Rule 50.13).

**Case Conference Procedures:**
- Scheduled via Calendly for major locations
- Presumptive mode: Zoom (family proceedings)
- Associate judges authorized to make orders
- No separate judge's order required
- Timetable and directions established

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Create case conference scheduling interface
- Integrate with Calendly
- Track case conference dates and outcomes
- Generate case management orders
- Maintain case management timetable
- Generate case conference compliance reports

### 6.2 First Judicial Pre-Trial Conference

The first judicial pre-trial conference is a critical step in case management. The system must track this and ensure compliance with regional procedures.

**First Judicial Pre-Trial Conference Objectives:**
- Preferred attendance: Counsel and clients
- Discuss resolution of proceeding
- If settlement not achieved, judge/associate judge shall order:
  - Exchange of Affidavits of Documents
  - Dates for examinations
  - Dates for answering undertakings
  - Dates for subsequent pre-trial conference
  - Other necessary directions

**Implementation Requirements:**
- Track first pre-trial conference date
- Generate pre-trial conference brief template
- Enforce 15-page limit for briefs
- Track orders and timetable
- Generate pre-trial conference compliance reports
- Maintain pre-trial conference history
